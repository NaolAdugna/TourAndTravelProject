WESTERN ROUTES -------

Abijata-Shalla Lakes National Park

Located in the Great Rift Valley, Abijata-Shalla Lakes National Park is home to two scenic crater lakes - Lake Abijata and Lake Shalla- that attract thousands of flamingos and other bird species making it a great spot for bird watching.

Blue Nile Falls

The Blue Nile Falls, also known as Tis Abay in Amharic, is one of Ethiopia's most popular tourist attractions. It is located near Bahir Dar on the Blue Nile River and is an impressive sight with water cascading down a 45-meter drop.

Gambella National Park

The Gambella National Park is one of Ethiopia's hidden natural gems, located in western Ethiopia close to South Sudan border. Visitors can enjoy an off-road safari to see animals such as zebras, elephants, baboons and hippos among others.

Dhidessa River

The Dhidessa River is a major river in western Ethiopia that flows through the regions of Oromia and Benishangul-Gumuz. With a total length of about 860 km, the Didessa River is one of the most important tributaries of the Blue Nile.
